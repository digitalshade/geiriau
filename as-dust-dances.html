As Dust Dances

Underneath the soles of passers by, leaving their trails behind them, the white bed compresses and crunches. Along the street and over the broken kerbs, above the cracks in the pavements. Only footprints remain where the untouched blanket once lay.

And above it all a single light hangs, illuminating the black night. Through rays of tungsten the dust falls.

It twists and turns and floats in the air, swirls and glides along, a passenger on the current of a cool night's breeze. It rises and falls, like puffs of flour, gradually dropping, sinking to the concrete below, making secrets of the trails and sprinkling a new bed.

From inside they watch the movements, huddling for each other's warmth, in silence and awe. They smile and they wonder, their eyes unwavering, unblinking at the motion.

And outside, among the shadows, dust dances in the streetlights.
